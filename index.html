About Shannon

Hello there! My name is Shannon and I am a professional aerialist with over 8 years performing and teaching experience. I have numbers on aerial straps, aerial silks, and a duo act on double loops. Performing highlights include performing my silks number at the Wintergarten, Berlin’s oldest variete theater, and hanging underneath a motorcycle on a tight wire with the Flying Espanas. I have also participated as a facilitator of circus, judging aerial competitions and assisting backstage on the internationally acclaimed German show SOAP.

I have taught workshops and classes to all ages and abilities in 17 States from Circus Smirkus in Vermont, all the way to the legendary San Fransisco Circus Center in California. Internationally in Mexico, Canada, and Europe. My mission is to demand proper execution of technique while at the same time giving the students ownership over their individual creative process.

  • Teacher Training at The New England Center for Circus Arts, Montreal, and Guadalajara
  • BFA in experimental theater at New York University
  • 5 years instruction and performing all over the world

My three most popular workshops are Straps For Aerialists, Drop, Stop, & Roll (Fabric Drop Workshop), and Intro to Spanish Web

​
Straps For Aerialists
This workshop uses aerial straps to address fundamental movements and muscle activation for any aerial apparatus. We will explore concepts for smoother inversions, comfortable one-arm positions, and shoulder stabilization exercises. The goal is to learn to use the straps as a conditioning tool for any aerialists to gain greater mobility and strength.
Drop, Stop, & Roll (Fabric Drop Workshop)
As aerialists, we seek control over our bodies at every moment we are in the air. When we are climbing and posing, this control is relatively easy to master. However, it gets more complicated when we begin to look at “drops.” In this workshop we will look at several basic drops which cause us to rotate forward or sideways. We will explore how the wrap, the body positioning in the set up, and the body positioning during the drop can change the speed and shape of the skill. This clinic is great for those who are looking to clean up their drops and ensure they are executing these rotations in the safest way.
Intro to Spanish Web
Love to spin? Spanish web is for you! A Spanish web is a rope with a hand loop attached to it. The aerialist hangs by the wrist or the ankle in the loop fluidly changing positions while spinning through the air. We will learn not only the art of being spun, but also the important job of “setting,” or being the person below who generates the spin. Don't miss the opportunity try this traditional circus apparatus!
